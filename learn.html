Crypto Glossary

Quick reference for common cryptocurrency terms.

Blockchain

A distributed ledger technology that records transactions across multiple computers in a way that makes it difficult to alter retroactively. Each block contains a cryptographic hash of the previous block, creating a chain.

Wallet

A digital tool that allows users to store, send, and receive cryptocurrencies. Can be hot (online) or cold (offline) storage. Contains public and private keys for accessing your crypto.

Gas Fees

Transaction fees paid to blockchain validators for processing and confirming transactions. Varies based on network congestion and transaction complexity. Measured in Gwei on Ethereum.

Staking

The process of locking up cryptocurrency to support blockchain network operations and earn rewards. Used in Proof-of-Stake consensus mechanisms as an alternative to mining.

NFT

Non-Fungible Token - a unique digital asset that represents ownership of a specific item or piece of content on the blockchain. Unlike cryptocurrencies, each NFT is unique and cannot be exchanged 1:1.

DEX

Decentralized Exchange - a peer-to-peer marketplace where users can trade cryptocurrencies directly without intermediaries. Uses smart contracts to facilitate trades and maintain custody of funds.

HODL

Originally a misspelling of "hold," now means to hold cryptocurrency long-term regardless of price volatility. A buy-and-hold investment strategy popular in the crypto community.

Fork

A change to the blockchain protocol that creates a divergence from the original chain. Can be "hard" (incompatible with older versions) or "soft" (backward compatible).

Market Cap

Market Capitalization - the total value of a cryptocurrency, calculated by multiplying the current price by the circulating supply. Used to rank and compare different cryptocurrencies.
